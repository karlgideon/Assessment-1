Chicken Adobo Recipe

Adobo is a popular dish that is treasured in Filipino cuisine and throughout the world. It's known for its savory, tangy, and totally unique blend of flavors.


Ingredients:

Prep time: 5 minutes | Cook time: 35 minutes | Total time: 40 minutes | Servings: 4 people

  • 2 lbs chicken (cut into serving pieces — thighs and drumsticks work best)
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 6 tbsp white vinegar (or cane vinegar)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 pcs bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorn
  • 1½ cups water
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1¼tsp salt (to taste)

Method:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, and crushed garlic. Mix well. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight is even better for deeper flavor).

  2. Pan-Fry for Extra Flavor: Heat oil in a pan. Lightly pan-fry the marinated chicken for about 2 minutes per side. This helps lock in flavor and improves texture.

  3. Simmer the Adobo: Pour in the marinade and add water. Bring to a boil. Toss in bay leaves and whole peppercorns. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the chicken is nice and tender

  4. Add the Vinegar: Pour in the vinegar. Don’t stir right away — let it boil for a few minutes to cook off the sharpness. Then simmer uncovered for another 10 minutes.

  5. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed: Add a bit of sugar to balance the flavors, and salt if needed. You can continue simmering uncovered to reduce the sauce if you like it thick and rich.

  6. Serve and Enjoy!: Serve the adobo over steamed rice and enjoy!
